The Lightning P-38 with belt drive

Gates has been making their Carbon Drive system for a number of years, but in the past limited belt selection prevented their use on recumbent bicycles.

But now Gates is making a large number of belt sizes, and the committed engineers at Lightning Cycle Dynamics went back to the drawing boards and designed a new P-38 type frame with ability to accept the belts as the world’s first commercial belt drive recumbent!

Coupling belts with a smooth shifting, precision-machined, internally-geared Rohloff 14-speed hub results in a super quiet, maintenance-free recumbent ride.

Read the Recumbent & Tandem Rider Magazine test drive!

Attention

  1. Because the X-seam dimension can change based on seat angle, crankarm length, and shoe thickness, nominal X-seam means average or median of these factors.
  2. Seat bolt to crank center distance is a much more accurate method of determining the belts needed. This can be obtained by sitting on and correctly adjusting an existing P-38 frame.
  3. Nominal gearing is 20 rear, 24 middle, and 42 or 50 front. If using a 50 front the number of belt teeth in the above chart needs to be increased by a total of 4 teeth.
  4. To achieve these dimensions, the gearing needs to be 22 middle and 39 or 46 front.
